What is an owner's representative?

Contractors negotiate contracts every day.
Most homeowners do it once, then move on.

That difference in frequency is where scope gets loose, costs grow, and leverage shifts. I get involved at the moments that matter most, so you are never walking into a critical decision without someone who has seen it before.

I scope the work before anyone bids it. I review bids line by line. I verify the work matches what you paid for. I build you a punch list before you release final payment so you close with leverage, not a handshake.

Common questions

Straight answers before you decide

Why add another expense to the project?

A problem caught before work starts costs a conversation. The same problem caught after costs time, money, stress, and rework. I typically cost less than one change order.

How do I know you won't side with the contractor?

I have no financial relationship with any trade on your project. No referral fees. No subs I need to keep busy. My only interest is your outcome.

When in the process do I call you?

As early as possible. Before you have bids, before you have a contractor, ideally before you have a full picture of the work. The earlier I'm involved, the more I can identify risk before it becomes cost.

I already have a contractor picked. Is it too late?

No. Start with the Scope and Bid Review. I'll verify their credentials, read the contract, and flag anything that exposes you before you sign. My services are relevant before and throughout your project.
